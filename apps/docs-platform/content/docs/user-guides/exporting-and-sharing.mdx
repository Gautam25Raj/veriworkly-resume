# Exporting & Sharing

VeriWorkly provides flexible ways to get your resume out of the editor and into the hands of recruiters. Because of our privacy-first architecture, the way you export dictates what happens to your data.

---

## 1. High-Fidelity PDF Export

Unlike traditional browser-based builders that rely on flawed client-side printing scripts, VeriWorkly utilizes a heavy-duty backend engine to generate pixel-perfect PDFs.

- **How it works:** When you click Export to PDF, your resume payload is sent to our API and placed in a Redis queue. A headless browser (Playwright) renders the document exactly as it appears on your screen and generates the PDF.
- **ATS Optimization:** Because the PDF is rendered properly as a digital document (not an image snapshot), all text remains selectable, and ATS parsers can read the exact semantic structure.

<Callout type="warn" title="Local-First Privacy Boundary">
  PDF generation requires sending your payload to the backend momentarily. However, if you are an
  unauthenticated user (no account), the data is only held in memory during generation, the PDF is
  returned, and the data is immediately discarded.
</Callout>

---

## 2. DOCX Export

If you need to manually tweak your resume in Microsoft Word or Google Docs, you can export your profile directly to a `.docx` file.

- **Client-Side Generation:** DOCX exports happen entirely within your browser using the `docx` library.
- **Formatting:** While the structural hierarchy (Headings, Bullet points) remains intact, the visual styling (colors, strict layouts) will default to standard Word formatting.
